Pest management

Welcome to our comprehensive Pest Management Services!

We understand that dealing with pest infestations can be a nuisance, and that's why we're here to provide effective solutions to ensure a pest-free environment in your home. With years of experience and a commitment to environmentally friendly practices, we offer a wide range of pest control options tailored to your specific needs.


At the Department of Agriculture, we follow the principles of integrated pest management (IPM) to address your pest problems. This approach combines both chemical and non-chemical solutions to ensure the most effective and eco-friendly pest control.


Our expert team specializes in the identification and management of a variety of crop-damaging pests commonly encountered on ecological farms, including but not limited to:


  • Aphids
  • Caterpillars
  • Weevils
  • Beetles
  • Mites
  • Thrips
  • Whiteflies
  • Leafhoppers
  • Rodents (if applicable)

If these pests are causing damage to your crops on your ecological farm, we have the solution. Please note that we primarily focus on outdoor and crop-damaging pests and do not handle indoor pest issues.

Seed Certification


Welcome to our comprehensive Seed certification Services!

Our Seed Certification program is your gateway to ensuring the highest quality seeds for your agricultural ventures. With our rigorous testing and certification process, you can trust that the seeds you plant are of the utmost quality, setting the foundation for a bountiful harvest. Join us in preserving the integrity of organic seeds and promoting biodiversity in agriculture.

We strive to achieve agricultural development through quality control of seed and planting material facilitation of import/ export of quality plant and plant products, prevention of dangerous pests within the country, regulation of pesticides, conservation of genetic resources of agricultural crops and enforcement of seed, plant protection and control of pesticides acts for protection of local agriculture and environment to ensure food security of the nation.
We regulate under the following Acts in relation to this sector of economical agriculture:


  • Plant Protection Act No. 35 of 1999 to protect the agriculture and promote effective pest management strategies with least harm to the environment.
  • Control of Pesticide Act No. 33 of 1980 to ensure availability of high quality pesticide with least hazard to human health and the environment.
  • Seed Act No. 22 of 2003 to safeguard the farmers as well as the seed handlers for malpractices that would harm the seed industry.

The Seed Certification Service of the Department of Agriculture was formally established in 1979 with the assistance of the Netherland Government Aid program. However the DOA has provided the service of Seed testing since 1958 during said year 1758 samples of paddy were tested. A seed testing laboratory with an annual capacity of 5000 samples was established at Peradeniya in 1970.


Our seed certification services include;


  • Certification of seed paddy.
  • Certification of vegetable seeds.
  • Certification of Other field crops.
  • Certification of seed potato.
  • Certification of fruit plant nurseries.
  • Registration of fruit plant nurseries and mother plants of fruits.
  • Certification of breeder seeds.

Organic Certification


Welcome to our comprehensive Organic certification Services!

We assist farmers with their unique organic certification needs through comprehensive technical assistance, events, resources and more.
Our team of experts will guide you through the steps and requirements of organic certification.

Organic-certified products are in high demand. By going organic, you can enhance the market value of your produce.

Our trained inspectors visit your farm to evaluate its adherence to organic standards. They assess factors like soil health, pest management, and crop rotation.
